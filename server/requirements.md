The proposed system should allow librarians to manage book records, member details, book issue and return transactions, fine calculation, and book purchase management including purchase orders, supplier records, invoice entry, and stock updates. The system should also enable students to search for books ( PLACE), check availability, view borrowing history, and receive due-date reminders. By implementing this system, the library can improve accuracy, save time, reduce manual workload, ensure proper financial and inventory management, and provide a better learning and research environment.
Objectives
1.	To develop a computerized system for managing library books, members, and transactions efficiently.
2.	To automate the process of book issue, return, and renewal to reduce manual work and errors.
3.	To maintain accurate and up-to-date records of all books, including availability status.
4.	To manage student and staff membership details in a structured and secure manner.
5.	To implement fine calculation for overdue books automatically.
6.	To enable fast searching of books by title, author, subject, or ISBN.
7.	To maintain a proper book purchase management system, including purchase orders, vendor details, invoices, and stock updates.
8.	To generate useful reports such as issued books, overdue lists, stock summary, and purchase history.
9.	To improve inventory control and ensure proper tracking of damaged, lost, or replaced books.
10.	To enhance the overall efficiency, accuracy, and service quality of library operations.
